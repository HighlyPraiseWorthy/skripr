Three things Skripr's palette does well — and one risk

Bandwidth — Violet sits in the "creative AI" quadrant. Creators see it and think "sophisticated tool, not bro-marketing." It's the same colour Vercel, Supabase, Linear, and every generative-AI brand uses to signal competence.

Per-segment differentiation — The 8 feature gradients (niche bend ⇆ violet, compliance ⇆ green, title gen ⇆ amber, metadata ⇆ cyan, remixer ⇆ red) give each tool its own visual identity within the same brand family. Users can learn the colour→tool mapping subconsciously.

Low fatigue on dark mode — Dark background + violet-on-black is widely perceived as "pro" by developers and creators. The palette is easy on the eyes during long dashboard sessions.

The risk — Violet is calm. It doesn't push. On the top banner's "2 scripts free" CTA, violet is polite where red is insistent. If your primary conversion lever is the banner → sign-up → paid upgrade path, the current palette silently leaves conversion on the table in that one spot.
